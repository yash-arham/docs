The **404** page in the Sentiment theme is built from two sections: a **Page not found** section with a branded error display, a site search bar, and quick-navigation links, and a **Product strip** that surfaces a curated selection to keep customers engaged rather than bouncing. Both sections are fully configurable from the Theme Editor.

<Frame>
  ![404](/images/404.png)
</Frame>

### How to Configure the 404 Page

1. From your Shopify admin, go to **Online Store → Themes** and click **Customize**.
2. From the top page dropdown, select **404**.
3. Click **Page not found** or **Product strip** in the left sidebar under **Template** to open its settings.
4. Click **Save** when done.

The 404 page template contains these sections:

- **Page not found** — branded error display with eyebrow, headline, opening paragraph, search bar, and quick-navigation links
- **Product strip** — editorial product grid with heading, view-all link, and collection source

---

### Page Not Found Section Settings

The Page Not Found section is the core of the 404 experience — it renders a two-column layout with a large animated "404" graphic on the left and the headline, body text, search bar, and quick links on the right.

<Frame>
  ![404 1 1](/images/404-1-1.webp)
</Frame>

#### Content

- **Eyebrow label:** Small label displayed above the headline (e.g., "Lost & found").
- **Headline (normal text):** The regular-weight portion of the main headline (e.g., "This page was").
- **Headline (italic / accent text):** The italic or accent-colour portion of the headline rendered in the theme's accent typeface (e.g., "melted down.").
- **Opening paragraph:** Supporting body text displayed below the headline (e.g., "Nothing here is ever wasted — and the link you followed has long since returned to the loop. Search the house below, or pick up one of the trails that still lead somewhere.").

#### Search

- **Search placeholder:** Placeholder text inside the search input (e.g., "Search the house...").
- **Search button label:** Label on the search submit button (e.g., "Search").

#### Quick Links

- **Links label:** Small uppercase label displayed before the quick-link buttons (e.g., "Go to").

### Product Strip Section Settings

The Product Strip section renders a static product grid below the 404 message — keeping customers engaged by surfacing relevant pieces from a collection alongside an editorial heading and a view-all link.

<Frame>
  ![404 2 2](/images/404-2-2.webp)
</Frame>

#### Layout

- **Enable full width:** Toggle to extend the section edge-to-edge across the viewport.

#### Content

- **Eyebrow label:** Small label displayed above the heading (e.g., "While you're here").
- **Heading (normal text):** The regular-weight portion of the section heading (e.g., "Pieces that").
- **Heading (italic text):** The italic portion of the section heading rendered in the theme's accent typeface (e.g., "wandered off").
- **Typography:** Choose the typographic preset for the heading (e.g., Heading 2).
- **View all link text:** Label for the view-all link displayed to the right of the heading (e.g., "View the full index").
- **View all link URL:** The destination URL for the view-all link (e.g., `/collections/all`).

#### Products

- **Source collection:** Select the Shopify collection to pull products from (e.g., Art Ring).
- **Number of products:** How many products to display in the grid (range: 2–8).
- **Columns:** Number of columns on desktop — 2, 3, or 4.

#### Spacing

- **Padding top / Padding bottom:** Control vertical spacing around the section.
